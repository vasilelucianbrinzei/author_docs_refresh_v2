# Understand the WMS Lifecycle

## Introduction

Use this page for a high-level view of how a workshop moves through Oracle Workshop Management System (WMS). It is meant to orient authors before they work through the detailed WMS, QA, and publish labs.

Estimated Time: 10 minutes

### Objectives

* Understand the main WMS statuses in order
* Know which roles act at each stage
* Know which detailed lab to open next

## Task 1: Understand The Status Flow

1. `Submitted`: the workshop request has been created and is waiting for council review.
2. `More Info Needed`: the council needs clarification before approving the workshop. The workshop team responds in **Message the Team**.
3. `Approved`: the request is accepted and the workshop team can begin active development.
4. `In Development`: the workshop team is building the workshop in GitHub and keeping WMS details current.
5. `Self QA`: the workshop team is validating the content and completing the Self QA checklist.
6. `Self QA Complete`: the workshop team has certified the checklist and stakeholders are expected to review the workshop.
7. `Completed`: stakeholders have accepted the workshop and it is ready for publish requests.
8. `Quarterly QA` and `Quarterly QA Complete`: published workshops cycle through scheduled maintenance and verification.
9. `Disabled`: the workshop can be removed from production if required maintenance is not completed in time.

## Task 2: Know Who Acts At Each Stage

1. The council reviews new requests and decides whether the workshop should move forward.
2. The workshop team authors the content, updates WMS metadata, completes self-QA, and responds to open questions.
3. Stakeholders verify the quality of the finished workshop before it moves to `Completed`.
4. The LiveLabs publishing team reviews publish requests and pushes approved content to production.
